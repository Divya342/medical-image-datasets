Welcome

Welcome to the Medical Image Dataset Links website, your go-to resource for accessing various medical image datasets available online. Our goal is to facilitate the search for datasets for researchers, developers, and medical professionals working on healthcare projects.

Popular Medical Image Datasets

  • The Cancer Imaging Archive (TCIA)

    A large archive of medical images for research. TCIA offers a vast array of imaging data for cancer research, including CT, MRI, and PET scans. It supports the development of advanced diagnostic tools and cancer treatment methods.

  • OASIS

    Open Access Series of Imaging Studies, neuroimaging datasets. OASIS provides MRI data for studying aging and Alzheimer's disease. The datasets include structural and functional brain images from healthy and diseased individuals.

  • Medical Segmentation Decathlon

    A collection of medical imaging datasets for segmentation tasks. This challenge dataset includes various imaging modalities and anatomical structures, promoting the development of robust and generalizable segmentation algorithms.

  • NIH Clinical Center

    A large dataset of chest X-rays with disease labels. This dataset is crucial for developing models for detecting diseases like pneumonia and tuberculosis. It includes over 100,000 frontal-view X-ray images of patients with 14 different thoracic diseases.

  • MURA Dataset

    Musculoskeletal radiographs. MURA is a large dataset for bone X-rays, useful for developing diagnostic models. It includes images of upper extremity radiographs, helping improve the accuracy of bone fracture detection systems.

  • LIDC-IDRI

    Lung Image Database Consortium image collection. LIDC-IDRI offers a comprehensive set of lung CT scans with annotated nodules for cancer research. It supports the development of computer-aided diagnosis (CAD) systems for lung cancer detection.

  • Human Connectome Project (HCP)

    Neuroimaging data. HCP provides high-quality MRI data for mapping the human brain's structural and functional connectivity. The project aims to understand the neural pathways that underlie human brain function.

About
